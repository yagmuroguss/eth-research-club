# Name
The name of the organization is ETH Research Club.

# Purpose
The club aims to: 
- Foster research skills and encourage members to engage in scientific research.
- Promote scientific literacy and critical thinking in an academic context.
- Facilitate connections between students and academic mentors.
- Encourage collaborative research.
- Provide opportunities to apply theoretical knowledge in practice through seminars and workshops.
- Support members in transitioning into academic research environments and higher education pathways.

# Membership
- Membership is open to all students in ETH Zürich.
- Members are expected to actively participate in their research project, if they have one.
- Membership may be revoked in case of repeated misconduct or violation of rules.

# Structure
The club consists of:
- President: Responsible for overall coordination of the Club and final decision-making in case of tie votes.
- Vice president: Works in coordination with the President on all club operations.
- Event Coordinator(s): Organizes seminars, workshops, and other academic events.
- Media Team: Responsible for promoting the Club, managing social media accounts, and publishing content on the website.
- Finance Team: Manages financial matters, including budgeting, expenses, and sponsorship-related activities.
- Education Team: Finds and coordinates with academic advisors and mentors.
- Membership Team: Recruits new members and supports onboarding processes.
- Members: Actively participate in Club activities, discussions, and projects. Members may also contribute to teams based on their interests.

# Meetings
- Regular meetings are held bi-weekly.
- Extraordinary meetings can be called by the President or at least 30% of members.

# Decision Making
- Decisions are made by a majority vote of the Board members and Heads.
- In case of a tie, the President has the casting vote.
- Major changes (e.g., statutes amendments) require a 2/3 majority.

# Activities
The Club may organize:
- Workshops
- Research discussion groups
- Project collaborations
- Guest lectures and seminars
- Paper presentations

# Removal of Officers
- If an officer repeatedly fails to fulfill their responsibilities or neglects their duties, they may be removed from their position by a vote.
- A removal decision requires a 2/3 majority vote of the members.
- The concerned person must be given the opportunity to explain themselves before the vote takes place.

# Amendments
- This statutes document can be amended by a 2/3 majority vote of members.
- Proposed amendments must be shared at least 3 days before voting.

# Dissolution
- The Club may be dissolved by an unanimous vote.
- In case of dissolution, remaining resources should be distributed for educational purposes.
