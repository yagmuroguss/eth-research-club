# Name
The name of the organization is ETH Computer Graphics Club.

# Purpose
The club aims to:
- Educate computer science students about computer graphics, 3D computing, and visualization.
- Lead student projects within the Club in various areas of computer graphics, including medical visualization, space visualization, educational applications, and web design.
- Connect students with industry professionals and researchers.

# Neutrality and Non-Profit Character
- The Club is politically and religiously neutral.
- The Club operates on a non-profit basis.

# Membership
- Membership is open to all students in ETH Zürich.
- Members are expected to actively participate in their project, if they have one.
- Membership may be revoked in case of repeated misconduct or violation of rules.

# Structure
The Club consists of:
- President: Responsible for the overall coordination of the Club and final decision-making in case of tie votes.
- Vice President: Works in coordination with the President on all Club operations.
- Event Coordination Team: Organize seminars, workshops, and other academic events.
- Media Team: Responsible for promoting the Club, managing social media accounts, and publishing content on the website.
- Finance Team: Manages financial matters, including budgeting, expenses, and sponsorship-related activities.
- Education Team: Finds and coordinates with academic advisors and mentors.
- Membership Team: Recruits new members and supports onboarding processes.
- Members: Actively participate in Club activities and projects.
- Advisors: Provide guidance and support to project groups throughout the development of their projects.

# Board
The Board consists of:
- President
- Vice President
- Head of Event Coordination
- Head of Media
- Head of Finance
- Head of Education
- Head of Membership

# Meetings
- Regular meetings are held bi-weekly.
- Extraordinary meetings can be called by the President or at least 30% of members.

# Decision Making
- Decisions are made by a majority vote of the Board members.
- In case of a tie, the President has the casting vote.
- Major changes (e.g., statutes amendments) require a 2/3 majority.

# Elections
- President and vice president are elected by a majority vote of the Board.
- Heads are elected by a majority vote of the members of their respective teams.

# Removal of Officers
- If an officer repeatedly fails to fulfill their responsibilities or neglects their duties, they may be removed from their position by a vote.
- A removal decision requires a 2/3 majority vote of the members.
- The concerned person must be given the opportunity to explain themselves before the vote takes place.

# Finances
- The Club may receive funding through university support, sponsorships, and donations.
- All financial resources must be used exclusively for Club activities and educational purposes.

# Official Language
- The official working language of the Club is English.

# Activities
The Club may organize:
- Workshops
- Project discussions
- Guest seminars
- Mentor meetings

# Amendments
- This statutes document can be amended by a 2/3 majority vote of members.
- Proposed amendments must be shared at least 3 days before voting.

# Dissolution
- The Club may be dissolved by an unanimous vote.
- In case of dissolution, remaining resources should be distributed for educational purposes.
